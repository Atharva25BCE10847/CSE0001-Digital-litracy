# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where scammers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased rapidly with the growth of digital payments.

Typically, the fraud begins when a scammer contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The scammer creates urgency, such as claiming a failed transaction or prize reward. Next, they send a fake payment request or malicious link and ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN or OTP. In some cases, victims are tricked into approving “collect requests” thinking they will receive money, but instead they send money.

College students and new digital users are often targeted because they may lack awareness about online fraud techniques. People who frequently use online shopping or payment apps are also vulnerable.

The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, mental stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. In some cases, victims are unable to recover their money.

To prevent such fraud, users must never share OTPs or UPI PINs, verify requests carefully, and report suspicious activity immediately.
